George Miller: A Significant Transition in Life

George Kusunoki Miller, also known as Joji, Pink Guy or Filthy Frank, is a Japanese singer, songwriter, rapper, record producer, one-time author, and former Internet personality and comedian.
George Miller started as an "entertainer/comedian," posting videos on his now defunct YouTube channels called DizastaMusic, TooDamnFilthy, and TVFilthyFrank. He used to post rap and parody songs, rants, extreme challenges and a bizarre show called The Filthy Frank Show. His Pink Guy alias, who is a zentai-wearing (tight onesie) character recurrs on the show, with his songs featured on the show and his discography spanning two full-length projects and an extended play. Pink Guy's album called Pink Season, topped the iTunes chart, #1 in the Hip Hop/Rap secntion and #2 in the main section.

The reason why I created this blog about Joji is that he greatly inspired me to create a significant transition/change in my life. If he can go from FilthyFrank, posting weird, extreme challenges and rants about stuff on the internet, and Pink Guy, a pink zentai-wearing, acting like a weird squirmy person in public, parody rap making guy, soaring the charts with his rap parody album, to Joji, this brilliant artist who creates music about life, love and heartaches, then I can do the same thing. His achievements and transition inspired me to become a better person than I was in the past and I wear my Hand of God necklace to remind myself to make a change.
